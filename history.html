History

A fifty-year story of academic cooperation, scientific exchange, and enduring partnership between Sri Lanka and the United States.

Historical Foundation of the Collaboration

The origins of this partnership can be traced to the early 1970s. What began as agricultural and medical education cooperation has evolved over five decades into a wide-ranging, multidisciplinary global collaboration.

Early
1970s

INTSOY Soybean Program — Agricultural Foundation

Sri Lanka benefited from international efforts led by the University of Illinois through the UNDP-supported INTSOY program, which introduced and promoted soybean cultivation. At a time when soybean was not widely cultivated in Sri Lanka, this initiative demonstrated its potential as a high-protein crop contributing to food security, agricultural diversification, and rural development.

1970s

UIC Medical Education Exchange

The University of Illinois Chicago (UIC), through its Department of Medical Education, contributed significantly to the development of modern medical education in Sri Lanka. Sri Lankan academics received advanced training at UIC and returned to establish foundational structures at the University of Peradeniya — leading to the creation of Sri Lanka's first formal Medical Education Unit, which influenced medical training practices across the country.

2012

Solar Energy Awareness Programs Begin

Multiple awareness and capacity-building programs were launched across Sri Lankan universities to promote solar energy technologies. Research into thin-film solar cell development and prototype manufacturing began, marking the expansion of the collaboration into the renewable energy sector.

2021–
Present

Sivananthan Laboratories Joins — Modern Research Phase

Over the past three years, the collaboration has been further strengthened through the active engagement of Sivananthan Laboratories Inc., led by distinguished University of Peradeniya alumnus Dr. Sivalingam Sivananthan. This phase expanded cooperation beyond traditional academic exchange into applied research, technology development, and innovation-driven initiatives in renewable energy, agriculture, and health sciences.

Recent

Revival of Agricultural Collaboration

Agricultural collaboration was revived through the introduction of improved soybean varieties and modern farming technologies in partnership with the University of Illinois Soybean Innovation Lab and the Sri Lankan Department of Agriculture. SMART FARM systems are being explored to improve productivity, sustainability, and resilience.

Recent

Expansion of Medical & Health Sciences Research

Medical and health sciences collaboration expanded significantly with faculty exchanges, training programs, and joint research initiatives across nephrology, neurology, mental health, immunology, surgery, and rehabilitation medicine. Research into chronic kidney disease of unknown etiology (CKDu) and AI in medical education emerged as key priorities.

2024–
Present

🏛️ Formal Center Proposed — PISCC Established

The Peradeniya–Illinois–Sivananthan Collaboration Center (PISCC) is formally proposed, transitioning fifty years of informal partnership into a structured, accountable, and scalable institutional framework — ensuring long-term sustainability, strategic growth, and global impact.
